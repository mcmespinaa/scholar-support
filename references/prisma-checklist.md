# PRISMA 2020 Checklist for Systematic Reviews

## Title
- [ ] Identify the report as a systematic review

## Abstract
- [ ] Structured summary: Background, Objectives, Methods, Results, Conclusions
- [ ] Include PROSPERO registration number if applicable

## Introduction
- [ ] Rationale: Describe the rationale for the review
- [ ] Objectives: Provide explicit statement of the questions being addressed (PICO)

## Methods
- [ ] Registration and protocol (PROSPERO number, protocol DOI)
- [ ] Eligibility criteria (inclusion/exclusion with rationale)
- [ ] Information sources (databases, registers, other sources, dates)
- [ ] Search strategy (full search for at least one database)
- [ ] Selection process (number of reviewers, how disagreements resolved)
- [ ] Data collection process (how data extracted, number of extractors)
- [ ] Data items (all outcomes, variables sought, assumptions)
- [ ] Study risk of bias assessment (tool used, how applied)
- [ ] Effect measures (for each outcome: RR, OR, MD, SMD, etc.)
- [ ] Synthesis methods (how studies grouped, statistical models, heterogeneity measures)
- [ ] Reporting bias assessment (methods used)
- [ ] Certainty assessment (e.g., GRADE approach)

## Results
- [ ] Study selection (PRISMA flow diagram with numbers at each stage)
- [ ] Study characteristics (citation, design, setting, population, outcomes, funding)
- [ ] Risk of bias in studies (results for each study)
- [ ] Results of individual studies (forest plots or tables)
- [ ] Results of syntheses (summary estimates, confidence intervals, heterogeneity)
- [ ] Reporting biases (results of assessment)
- [ ] Certainty of evidence (for each outcome)

## Discussion
- [ ] Discussion of results (context of other evidence)
- [ ] Limitations (study level and review level)
- [ ] Implications (practice, policy, future research)

## Other Information
- [ ] Registration and protocol (access details)
- [ ] Support/funding (sources, role of funder)
- [ ] Competing interests
- [ ] Availability of data/code/materials

## PRISMA-ScR Differences (Scoping Reviews)

Key adaptations for scoping reviews:
- Critical appraisal is **optional** (not mandatory)
- Replace "Synthesis methods" with "Charting methods"
- Replace "Effect measures" with description of how data was charted
- Focus on **mapping the extent** of evidence rather than synthesising effects
- Results typically presented as **thematic categories** or conceptual maps
- No requirement for meta-analysis or certainty of evidence assessment
